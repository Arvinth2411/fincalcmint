    About FinCalc Mint

    Free financial calculators built for every Indian

    Who We Are

    FinCalc Mint is a free online financial calculator platform built specifically for Indian users. We offer a comprehensive suite of tools covering home loan EMI, car loan, SIP returns, fixed deposit maturity, GST, salary take-home, income tax (new vs old regime), inflation, and loan comparison — all in one place.

    Our mission is simple: make financial planning accessible to every Indian, whether you're a salaried employee trying to understand your take-home pay, a first-time home buyer planning your EMI, or an investor calculating SIP returns.

    Our Principles

    • 100% Free, Always: Every calculator on this site is completely free to use with no hidden charges, no premium tier, and no account required.
    • Privacy First: All calculations happen entirely in your browser. We do not collect, store, or transmit any of your financial data. Your numbers stay on your device.
    • Accuracy: We use the same standard formulas used by Indian banks and the Income Tax Department. Our EMI formula matches RBI guidelines; our tax calculations reflect the latest Union Budget announcements.
    • India-Focused: Every detail — from INR formatting in lakh/crore notation to Indian bank rate benchmarks to GST slabs — is designed specifically for the Indian financial context.
    • No Ads That Harm UX: We run Google AdSense ads to keep the site free, but we place them carefully so they never block calculator inputs or results.

    Accuracy & Limitations

    While we strive for accuracy, all results from FinCalc Mint are indicative estimates. Actual values may vary depending on your bank's exact processing, rounding methods, fee structures, and applicable tax rules. We strongly recommend verifying results with your bank, employer, or a qualified chartered accountant before making financial decisions.

    Tax calculations are based on publicly announced the latest Union Budget rules. Surcharges for high-income earners, special rate incomes, and complex tax situations are not fully covered by this tool.
